Welcome to the website of the Office of Information Practices (OIP), whose mission is “ensuring open government while protecting individual privacy.” OIP administers two laws to promote open and transparent government in Hawai’i: the Uniform Information Practices Act (UIPA) , HRS Chapter 92F, which requires open access to government records, and the Sunshine Law , part I of HRS Chapter 92, which requires open public meetings. Both laws are intended to open up governmental processes to public scrutiny and participation by requiring government business to be conducted as transparently as possible, while balancing personal privacy rights guaranteed under the Hawaii State Constitution. OIP provides uniform interpretation, advice, and training on these laws to nearly all of Hawaii’s state and county agencies and boards and to the general public. OIP renders advice and assistance on questions concerning the public’s right to access to government records or meetings, and also provides training to help agencies comply with the laws. Although the public has the right to go to court without having to involve OIP, it is not necessary to hire attorneys or observe judicial formalities to obtain OIP’s assistance, and OIP’s free and informal proceedings are not subject to the contested case procedures of HRS Chapter 91. To explain the open government laws’ requirements, OIP has training tools and guides readily available on this website. Training materials and OIP’s annual reports are posted on this website and there are links to other open government agencies around the world. In the What’s New section, OIP regularly provides updates on its activities and has links to open government news from around the world. To begin or stop receiving OIP’s e-mailed What’s New updates, please e-mail [email protected] . Through OIP’s Attorney of the Day service, members of the public or government agencies can e-mail [email protected] or call (808) 586-1400 to receive (usually within 24 hours) general, non-binding advice regarding the UIPA or Sunshine Law. If further action is necessary, OIP may conduct an investigation. When access to a public record has been denied by a government agency, the requester may file an appeal with OIP. OIP also renders formal or informal opinions, which are enforceable by the courts. OIP’s opinions, including a searchable subject matter index for UIPA Opinions , and a searchable subject matter index for Sunshine Law Opinions , can be found on this website, along with the laws , rules , and various forms . In addition to the UIPA and Sunshine Law, OIP administers the Records Report System (RRS) , a computerized database (without the actual records) describing the more than 29,000 record titles of the various types of government records maintained by state and county agencies that may be available for public access. OIP also trains and assists agencies to use the UIPA Record Request Log and uploads their Log summaries to a government-wide support group on the State’s centralized open data website at opendata.hawaii.gov . Through this website, you have access to a wealth of open government information and OIP is able to cost-effectively and efficiently share the knowledge of its experienced staff members to encourage greater awareness of and compliance with Hawaii’s UIPA and Sunshine Law. For an overview of the website’s features, click on this link . Thank you for visiting OIP’s website and we hope that you will find most of the answers to your open government questions here. If you require an auxiliary aid or accommodation due to a disability, please contact (808) 586-1400 or e-mail [email protected] . OIP will strive to return your initial call or e-mail by the end of the next business day. Overview of OIP’s Website at oip.hawaii.gov 
Visitors to the OIP site can access, among other things, the following information and materials: 
What’s New at OIP and in open government news  
The UIPA and the Sunshine Law statutes  
OIP’s administrative rules  
Important legislation and legislative histo ry 
 OIP’s formal opinion letters – f ull tex t and summaries 
Formal opinion letter subject index  
Informal opinion letter summaries 
Informal opinion letter subject index 
Training guides, presentations, and other materials for the UIPA, Sunshine Law, and              
Appeals to OIP  
General guidance for commonly asked questions  
OIP’s annual reports 
 Model forms created by OIP  
Guides and links to the Records Report System  
Links to the State and County Calendars, data.hawaii.gov, a nd other good government 
agencies and websites in Hawaii and elsewhere 
O
IP’s website at oip.hawaii.gov features nine main pages that can be accessed through links 
placed immediately below the state seal on the home page.   The most frequently used pages can 
also be accessed through four large box links located on the right of the home page (What’s 
New, Laws/Rules/Opinions, Training, and Contact Us).  A brief description of each main page follows.
 “What’s New” 
OIP’s frequent What’s New articles provide helpful tips and current news regarding OIP and 
open government issues. To be included on OIP’s What’s New e- mail list, please e -mail 
oip@hawaii.gov.  
“Laws/ Rules/ Opinions”  
The Laws/Rules/Opinions tab  has a d rop down menu that will take you to the following pages : 
UIPA : a link to the complete text of the UIPA, with quick links to each section of the 
statute .  Also, link s to the Rules page; the UIPA Guide and training videos, along with 
additional guidance and forms ; UIPA Record Request Log training and instructions; and 
information about appeals to OIP.  
Sunshine Law : a link to the complete text of the Sunshine Law, with quick links to each 
section  of the statute .  Also, link s to the Rules page; the Sunshine Law Guides and 
training videos, along with additional guidance and forms; and information about appeals 
to OIP.  
Rules : links to OIP’s  administrative rules for appeals to OIP in Sunshine Law and UIPA 
cases , and the complet e text of OIP’s administrative rules for agency procedures and f ees 
to process  record  requests  made under the UIPA.  Also provided are links to the Impact 
Statement s, testimony, guides, and other materials relating to the rules, as well as Draft 
Rules bein g considered by OIP that would amend the current rules. 
Opinions: this page has three sections, with explanations and links to the following: 
Formal Opinions : link to the Formal Opinion Letter Summaries and Full Text 
page, as well as links to the UIPA subject matter index; the Sunshine Law subject matter index; a chronological list of all OIP formal opinion letters ; and a table of 
statutes and rules cited in OIP opinion letters.   
Informal Opinions : link to OIP’s Informal Opinion Letter Summaries page, 
where there are links to either Sunshine Law or UIPA informal opinions.  
Court Opinions :  links to a list of important court opinions and to selected 
opinions or OIP summaries/analyses of opinions. “Legislation” 
The Legislation page contains links to two other pages for (1) important pending and recent 
legislation and (2) legislative history of the UIPA, Sunshine Law, and other significant statutes. 
“Training”  
The training page provides all of  OIP’s training  materials and recent upda tes.  There are training 
sections for the UIPA and Sunshine Law, as well as links to court opinions and explanatory 
materials about  appeals t o OIP. 
“Forms” 
The Forms page provides model forms created by OIP to facilitate requests for gove rnment 
records  and agency/board complianc e with the Sunshine Law and the UIPA, including the UIPA 
Record Request Log.  
“Reports” 
The Reports page has a drop down menu for (1) OIP’s annual reports, (2)  OIP’s r eports 
summarizing data from the state and county ag encies’ UIPA Record Req uest Year-End Logs, 
and (3) special reports.  
“Records Report System (RRS)” 
This page explains and has guides and links to the Records Report System for the public and for 
agencies .  
“State Calendar and Related Links”  
This page contains helpful links to various external resources, starting with websites in Hawaii.  
Here, you will find links to the State and County C alendar s showing the meeting agendas for all 
state or county boards.  There are also links to other state or county good government agency 
websites, as well as a link to Hawaii’s open data site at data.hawaii. gov, where the UIPA Master 
Record Request Log  results are posted by the state and county ag encies.  
To expand your search beyond Hawaii, you can also vi sit ot her U.S. federal and  state as w ell as 
international s ites concerning freedom of information, open data, and privacy protection.
